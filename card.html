Bald Eagle

Bald Eagles can dive and glide at speeds of over 100 miles per hour.

  • Scientific Name: Haliaeetus leucocephalus
  • Average Body Length: 86 cm
  • Average Wing Span: 2.1 m
  • Average Lifespan: around 20 years
  • Habitat: American wetland with coniferous or hardwood trees

The bald eagle is a bird of prey found in North America, with with dark brown plumage, a white head and tail, bright yellow beak, feet and irises.It is found near large bodies of open water with an abundant food supply and old-growth trees for nesting. The legs are feather-free, and the toes are short and powerful with large talons. The highly developed talon of the hind toe is used to pierce the vital areas of prey while it is held immobile by the front toes.
